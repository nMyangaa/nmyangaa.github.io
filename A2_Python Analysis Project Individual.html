## Creating Scatter plot of Temperature and Rentals

sns.lmplot(x   = 'Temperature',  
           y   = 'RENTALS',                  
           scatter  = True,       
           fit_reg  = True,       
           aspect   = 1.5,          
           data     = biker)         

# Title and Axes Labels
plt.title(label   = 'Temperature vs. Bike Rentals')
plt.xlabel(xlabel = 'Temperature (C)')
plt.ylabel(ylabel = 'Number of Rentals')
plt.xlim(left  = biker['Temperature'].min(), 
         right = biker['Temperature'].max())
plt.tight_layout(pad = 2)

# Displaying the Plot
plt.show(block = True)

2.3 Temperature vs. Bike Rentals

Since the correlation matrix suggested a strong link between temperature and rentals, it is important to note how, exactly. The graph shows that as temperature rises, rentals go up quickly, until roughly 20C, where there is a relative dropoff. The strongest increases occur in the mild to warm range, and very hot days show a slight decrease in use, most likely attributable to discomfort. This analysis of the temperature and rentals scatter plot confirms that there is an existence of a temperature comfort zone for riders. Furthermore, the article titled, "How Do Weather and Season Affect Bike Rentals?" by Bobby Chandra reinforces that the 15-25'C range is one that has shown the highest number of rides from customers (Chandra, 2021). Moving forward, the trends shown can guide and inform business decisions on promotions or pricing tied to weather.

3. Analysis Questions

When looking at the hourly average of bike rentals, it peaks around 7-9 AM and 46 PM, which are the typical commuting hours of work and school classes of the majority of individuals. It also shows that usage typically drops at night and in the early afternoon, which reinforces the fact that most rides are not recreationally used. Moreover, weekday rentals are consistently higher than weekends, which confirms that the bikes are used mainly for commuting. This is supported further by the fact that even in colder seasons, weekday rides remain strong regardless of weather conditions. Through these findings, we can conclude that the bikes are used as a tool primarily for commuting needs, in addition to recreational and casual use.

The scatter plot that illustrates the relationship between temperature and bike rentals can be utilized here. When looking at the plot, Temperature has the biggest effect on rentals with, by far, the strongest relationship with rentals, as demand rises until about 20C. For other related factors, rentals are also shown to be highest on dry, mild days that have low humidity levels. Weather conditions such as rain, snow, and high humidity can decrease bike usage. Furthermore, seasons Spring and Summer have the most rentals since these are seasons that have the weather sitting in the ideal range. This is also supported by the fact that clear days with few weather disturbances, most preferrably between 15C and 25C, encourage the riders for more usage.

When looking at all of the insights gleaned so far, they illustrate a clear path in optimizing strategies. Firstly, the active number of staff and bikes available should be concentrated around the mornings and evenings during workweeks to better serve customers while they are commuting. Secondly, promotions should targeted and sent out during dry, mild days with a mixture of loyalty offers and discounts. Promotional offers should also be utilized during the slower times of midday, to better seize customers wanting to travel. Thirdly, seasonal analyses show that bikes and possibly higher pricing can be justified during Summer when rentals are highest, while discounts and deals can help keep users active during the Winter months when rentals are lowest. Lastly, weather data like rain and humidity should guide real-time alerts and rider nudges on a frequent basis. Operations and marketing that is tied to timing and weather conditions will drastically improve availability, boost rentals, and increase customer satisfaction.

Bike rentals and bikesharing services remain a popular choice for individuals in all ranges of groups, but the overarching analyses illustrate that the rentals are primarily used for commuting to and from their place of work or studies. The significant timeframes where most rides occur have been identified to better promote marketing efforts, and weather, seasonal, and temperature analyses have been conducted to streamline operations more effectively. Based on these analyses, operations should be adjusted to match demand windows and maintenance should focus on keeping bikes ready during high-demand periods. Marketing and pricing should follow the same trends.
